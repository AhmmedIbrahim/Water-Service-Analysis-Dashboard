## 🚨 Problem Statement

Access to clean and reliable water remains a major challenge in many regions. Long waiting times, high contamination rates, and inefficient infrastructure planning negatively impact communities.

This project analyzes water service data to uncover key issues and provide **data-driven recommendations** to improve water accessibility, quality, and resource allocation.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

- 🌍 Analyze water accessibility across **urban vs rural** areas
- 🔍 Identify **dominant water sources**
- 🧪 Evaluate **water quality** (clean vs contaminated)
- ⏱️ Analyze **waiting times** for water access
- 💡 Provide **actionable recommendations**
- 💰 Support better **budget allocation**

---

## 📂 Dataset Description

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Province / Town | Geographic location |
| Population Served | Number of people per source |
| Water Source Type | Well, river, shared tap, home tap |
| Waiting Time | Minutes to access water |
| Water Quality | Clean / Contaminated |
| Suggested Improvements | Recommended interventions |
| Budget & Cost Data | Financial planning figures |

## 🔍 Key Insights

> ⚠️ **Critical findings from the analysis:**

| Metric | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| 👥 Rural Dependency | **60%+** of the population relies on rural water services |
| 🦠 Contamination Rate | **70%+** of water sources are contaminated |
| ⏳ Peak Waiting Time | Exceeds **240 minutes** during peak hours |
| 🪣 Common Sources | Wells and shared taps are the most prevalent |

---

## 💡 Recommendations

1. 💧 **Invest in water filtration systems** — deploy RO & UV filters in contaminated zones
2. 🏗️ **Improve rural infrastructure** — prioritize underserved communities
3. ➕ **Increase water access points** — reduce waiting times and overcrowding
4. 🎯 **Focus on high-contamination regions** — target interventions where risk is greatest
5. 📊 **Optimize budget allocation** — use data to prioritize spending effectively
